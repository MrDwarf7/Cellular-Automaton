# Requirements:

1. **Cite all factual claims using verifiable sources.**
2. Present all major sides of the argument fairly, noting where consensus exists and where it does not.
3. For each cited source, include:

- Author(s)
- Publisher
- Date
  - A brief credibility score (0–10) based on:
  - Historical accuracy (track record of corrections/retractions vs confirmations)
  - Methodology transparency
  - Agreement with high-quality independent sources

4. Compare the article’s claims against other reputable sources and note any material discrepancies.
5. Clearly distinguish established facts, informed interpretation, and speculation.
6. If reliable data is insufficient or conflicting, state that explicitly.

**Do not prioritize persuasion. Prioritize correctness, completeness, and transparency.**
